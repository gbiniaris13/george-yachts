## Part 1. Wikipedia notability assessment (honest)

### The standard, plainly

English Wikipedia keeps an article only when the subject has received **significant coverage in multiple independent, reliable, secondary sources**. For companies and their founders the bar is higher than most people expect, governed by the dedicated guideline for organisations and companies. Three things matter, and all three must hold:

1. **Significant**, not passing. A few sentences, a quote, or a single mention inside a wider trend piece does not count as significant coverage of the company itself.
2. **Independent.** Press releases, wire syndications, the company's own site, interviews where the subject is doing the talking, and listings all fail the independence test. Coverage must be written *about* George Yachts by someone with no stake in it.
3. **Multiple reliable sources.** One excellent source is never enough. The community looks for several strong, independent pieces.

### Where George Yachts stands today

The honest answer: **below the bar, and not close enough to risk an article yet.**

- **Age.** The company was founded November 2025. It is roughly seven months old. New companies almost never clear notability, and reviewers treat very young firms with extra scepticism.
- **The Forbes feature.** The 1 May 2026 Forbes piece by Jacques Ledbetter, "How The Wealthy Are Hedging For Instability," is a genuine asset, but it has two weaknesses for notability purposes. First, it is a **contributor** byline. Wikipedia treats Forbes staff journalism as reliable but treats Forbes.com **contributor** posts as questionable, often unreliable, because they are not subject to the same editorial control. Second, George Yachts appears **inside a wider trend story**, as one example among several, not as the subject of the article. That is passing coverage, not significant coverage of the company.
- **IYBA membership.** Membership of a trade association is not coverage at all. It is a credential, useful for the body of an article, worthless for establishing notability.
- **Own data and the website.** The Greek Charter Index and georgeyachts.com are primary, self-published sources. They support facts once notability exists; they cannot establish it.

Net: today there is, at best, **one borderline source**. The guideline asks for **multiple strong ones**. An article created now would very likely be nominated for deletion and removed. A deleted article is a reputational mark, it is publicly logged, and re-creation later faces added friction. **Do not create the article yet.**

### The path to "yes" (and it is reachable)

The fix is not clever wording. It is **independent press**. George Yachts needs several substantial, independent pieces that are *about the firm or its founder*, not quotes inside someone else's story. Realistic, free or low-cost routes, in priority order:

1. **Yachting and charter trade press.** Aim for an outlet to write a profile or a market-view piece built around George Yachts and its Greek Charter Index data. Trade titles that practise real editorial review are strong sources. A data-led pitch ("Greek waters now ~30% of Mediterranean summer charter bookings, here is our 2026 read") is the kind of hook editors take.
2. **Greek national business and lifestyle press.** A founder profile or a Q&A-driven feature in a recognised Greek outlet (business daily, established lifestyle title) adds an independent, reliable source in a second language, which Wikipedia accepts.
3. **A second tier-one English placement with a staff byline**, ideally where George Yachts is the actual subject, not an example. This is the hardest and most valuable.
4. **Earned mentions that cite the Index as a primary market source.** Each time an independent journalist cites the Greek Charter Index by name and links the firm, that builds the citation record that notability rests on.

A practical readiness test: **three independent, reliable, substantial pieces in which George Yachts (or George P. Biniaris) is a principal subject, from at least two different publishers, at least one with a staff byline.** When that exists, revisit the article. Until then, hold the draft in Part 2 and keep the Wikidata item (which has no notability bar) accurate and rich.

A note on conflict of interest: when the time comes, George should **not** create or directly edit the article himself. The right move is to prepare a neutral, fully sourced draft (Part 2 is the starting point), submit it through Articles for Creation, and **disclose the connection** on the draft's talk page. That is the route least likely to be reverted or sanctioned.
