# Greece Captures Roughly 30 Percent of Mediterranean Summer Charter Bookings, George Yachts Reports in Inaugural Greek Charter Index 2026

### George Yachts publishes its first data-led market read on Greek crewed yacht charter, covering demand, booking lead times, all-in pricing, and the outlook into 2027.

KIFISIA, Athens, Greece, 28 June 2026 - Greek waters now account for roughly 30 percent of all Mediterranean summer charter bookings, making Greece the single most popular charter destination in the region, according to the Greek Charter Index 2026, the first compiled market read released today by George Yachts, the boutique luxury crewed yacht charter brokerage.

The Index draws on George Yachts' own booking and rate-card data across its curated fleet of motor, sailing, and catamaran yachts operating in the Cyclades, the Ionian, the Saronic, and the Dodecanese. It is published in full and free to read at georgeyachts.com/greek-charter-index-2026.

The headline finding reframes a question many advisers and principals ask each spring. Greece is no longer one option among several across the Mediterranean. On the brokerage's numbers, close to a third of the region's summer charters now take place in Greek waters, a concentration that reflects both the breadth of cruising grounds and the maturity of the local crewed-charter market.

Demand is also moving earlier in the calendar. George Yachts reports a typical booking lead time of 6 to 12 months for the peak July and August windows, with premium 40-metre-plus yachts frequently committed a year or more in advance. For clients who hold to fixed summer dates, the practical implication is that the most sought-after weeks are decided long before the season opens.

Within Greece, the demand map for 2026 is led by Mykonos and Santorini, while Milos is the fastest-rising island in the brokerage's bookings. The pattern points to a familiar core of marquee destinations holding their place at the top, alongside a quieter shift toward islands that reward guests willing to look slightly beyond the obvious.

The Index also addresses a subject the sector has historically left opaque, namely the true cost of a week aboard. George Yachts publishes an all-in framework that combines the base rate with the Advance Provisioning Allowance of about 33 percent, VAT at 13 percent, the reduced rate for commercial charters over 48 hours, and gratuity of about 17.5 percent. On that basis, an all-in week on a motor yacht runs from roughly EUR 45,000 for a vessel up to 25 metres in the shoulder season to about EUR 235,000 for a 40-metre-plus yacht at peak, with a 30-metre motor yacht costing approximately EUR 117,000 all-in for a peak-season week. A crewed sailing catamaran for 12 guests carries a weekly net base of EUR 15,000 to EUR 40,000 in the 2026 peak season. The full rate card is available at georgeyachts.com.

The 2026 picture is not uniformly buoyant. Motor-yacht demand softened materially over the year against a backdrop of conflict and elevated fuel prices. George Yachts reads this as a cyclical pause rather than a structural shift, and expects pent-up demand to return for the 2027 season.

"Greece has quietly become the default answer for a Mediterranean summer, and our own bookings make that plain," said George P. Biniaris, Founder and Managing Broker of George Yachts. "What our clients want now is not persuasion, it is clarity. They want to know what a week actually costs before they commit, and they want it early, because the best yachts for the best weeks are gone six to twelve months out. The motor-yacht market took a breath this year, and that is honest to say. The yachts that earn their keep will be the first to fill again in 2027, and the clients booking now are the ones setting the terms."

The Greek Charter Index 2026 is available to read and cite at georgeyachts.com/greek-charter-index-2026.
